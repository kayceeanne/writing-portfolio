feature articles

In Barangay Malis

An in-depth feature about the plights of residents in Barangay Malis, Guiguinto, Bulacan. Through interviews with community members, the article tackles the recurring issues of flooding, housing insecurity, and relocation amidst the construction of the North-South Commuter Railway (NSCR), a massive 147-kilometer urban rail transit system in the Philippines.

Tying the Knot, Breaking the Chain

As one of the only two countries where divorce is not legally allowed, the topic of divorce in the Philippines—from the belief that marriage is an inviolable social institution to growing discussions about couples and individuals trapped in loveless or abusive marriages—has made a shift in national conversation. This feature, with perspectives from government officials, the Church, and advocates, explores the legal, social, and cultural complexities surrounding the lack of divorce in the country.

Clicks for Kids: The Dangers of the Internet

With the rise of the “Sharenting” trend on TikTok, the dangers of online predators targeting kids have also grown exponentially. In this data-driven investigative feature, engagement patterns and public responses to videos featuring children were analyzed. Supported by insights from Violence Against Women and Children (VAWC) officials and parents, the article confronts the privacy and safety risks children face in the digital age.

When the Wheels on the Jeep Can't Go Round

Filipino jeepney drivers, following the implementation of the Public Utility Vehicle Modernization Program (PUVMP), were confronted with sudden and uncertain changes to their livelihoods. Through firsthand accounts and government perspectives, this feature explores the economic, social, and personal challenges brought about by the modernization program, as well as the resilience of drivers navigating a future with no clear destination.

The Cost of Passage: How residents of Barangay San Miguel built their way home

Flooding in the Philippines quickly became a national issue after allegations of corruption involving ghost flood control projects surfaced, alongside the worsening floods experienced across the country. One of the communities affected is Barangay San Miguel, where residents have had no choice but to rely on makeshift bridges built from scraps of wood and concrete to make their way home.

Holiday shopping bliss: Valenzuela's festive finds and local delights

Christmas bazaars in the Philippines are a common sight during the holiday season, where colorful booths and market stalls are as bright as the Christmas lights that line the streets. This seasonal news feature captures the vibrant atmosphere of a local holiday bazaar through the experiences of vendors and shoppers, while exploring the sense of community and the livelihoods the annual tradition continues to support.
